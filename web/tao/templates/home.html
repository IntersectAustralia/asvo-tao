Welcome!

The Theoretical Astrophysical Observatory (TAO) houses queryable data from multiple popular cosmological simulations and galaxy formation models. Your results can be funnelled through higher-level modules for additional enhancements and manipulation. TAO is accessible from anywhere you can access the internet.

If you haven't done so already, please register to use TAO. Under the hood, TAO is driven by the gSTAR supercomputer at the Swinburne University of Technology. Registering allows us to most efficiently manage the resources that TAO relies on.

If you are new to the world of Theoretical Astrophysical Observatories, please read our Introduction. In particular, you might want to browse our User's Guide and System Overview to learn about TAO's capabilities and how to get the best value from the system.

If you're ready to build a mock universe, go ahead and generate a new catalogue.

You can always access your past catalogues from the history tab.

BETA ALERT! TAO is currently in public beta and is undergoing rapid development. Right now not everything is stable or finished. Please report any unexpected behaviour or bugs so we can fix them. And always check that the data you downloaded is the data you expected before using it. All feedback and comments should be sent through our support form for the quickest possible response.

The Theoretical Astrophysical Observatory is a component of the All-Sky Virtual Observatory, which is a partnership between Astronomy Australia Ltd, Swinburne University of Technology, The Australian National University, the National Computational Infrastructure, and Intersect Australia Ltd.

The following organisations have assisted in funding TAO: The NeCTAR project; The Department of Industry, Innovation, Science, Research and Tertiary Education through AAL-managed Education Investment Fund (EIF) and National Collaborative Research Infrastructure Strategy (NCRIS) grants. For additional details, please see the ASVO Web Site.
